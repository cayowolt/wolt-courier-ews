This FAQ explains how the Early Warning System works, how each metric is defined, and how to interpret the watchlist. The system is designed to surface retention risk 4–6 weeks before it appears in standard M1 KPIs — giving market teams enough time to intervene.
How does the Early Warning System work? ▼

Standard retention metrics (M1 active rate, M3 retention) are cohort-based and inherently lagging — by the time a drop appears, the affected cohort has already been lost. The EWS works by instrumenting the funnel events that predict retention rather than measuring retention itself.

Each week, the system measures 6 leading indicators for each market's newest courier cohort. Each indicator is compared against that market's 8-week rolling baseline and scored. The 6 scores are combined into a single composite health score (0–100). Markets below defined thresholds are flagged as Watch or Critical.

The typical lead time is 4–6 weeks: a drop in FTD conversion this week will show up in M1 retention in 3–4 weeks. This dashboard updates every Monday with the previous week's cohort data.

How is the Composite Score calculated? ▼
Score formula
Each of the 6 signals is scored 0–100 based on its deviation from the market's 8-week baseline. The composite score is a weighted average:
Score = 0.30 × FTD_Conversion + 0.20 × T7_Trips + 0.15 × FTD_Earnings + 0.15 × T14_Active_Hours + 0.10 × Shift_Cancellation (inverted) + 0.10 × Onboarding_Completion
Signal scoring (per signal)

Each signal's raw value is compared to its 8-week baseline. The per-signal score is:

  • 100 — at or above baseline
  • 50–99 — 1–15% below baseline (linear interpolation)
  • 0–49 — more than 15% below baseline (linear interpolation)

For inverse metrics (Shift Cancellation), the logic is reversed: values above baseline score low.

Signal definitions ▼
FTD Conversion Rate (%)

The percentage of newly activated couriers who complete their first delivery within 7 days of account approval. "Activated" means the courier has passed all onboarding steps and is eligible to receive orders.

Why it matters: The most predictive single metric for M1 retention. Couriers who don't complete a first delivery within 7 days have a <10% chance of being active at M1.

Lead time: 3–4 weeks ahead of M1 retention.

T7 Trips — Deliveries in first 7 days (median)

The median number of completed deliveries among couriers who completed at least one (i.e., excluding those who never converted). This measures how deeply new couriers engage once they start.

Why it matters: Couriers with 3+ trips in week 1 show 2× M3 retention vs. those with 1–2 trips. It captures whether couriers are building a working habit or just dipping their toes in.

Lead time: 5–6 weeks ahead of M3 retention.

FTD Earnings Quality (avg. €)

Average gross earnings from a courier's first delivery. This reflects the earnings experience of the very first trip — distance, pay per km, wait time at pickup, tip.

Why it matters: A bad first earnings experience is a churn signal disguised as a neutral event. Couriers whose first trip earns significantly below expectations are unlikely to return, even after successfully converting.

T14 Active Hours — Hours on shift in first 14 days

Total hours the courier spent with the app in "available" or "on delivery" state during their first two weeks. Measures engagement depth beyond trip count.

Why it matters: Distinguishes casual explorers from committed couriers. Couriers with ≥15 active hours in their first 14 days have materially better M1 and M3 retention curves.

Shift Cancellation Rate (%)

The percentage of scheduled or committed shifts that were cancelled by the courier (in markets with scheduled supply) or the percentage of active sessions ended within 15 minutes of starting (in open-market regions). Measured on the current week's new cohort.

Why it matters: Rising cancellations are a behavioural pre-churn signal — couriers who are disengaging tend to start accepting shifts then abandoning them 2–3 weeks before they go inactive.

Onboarding Completion Rate (%)

The percentage of new signups who complete all required onboarding steps (document upload, profile completion, equipment pickup if applicable) within 7 days of signup.

Why it matters: Onboarding friction reduces the FTD conversion pool before a courier ever attempts a delivery. A drop in onboarding completion will show up in FTD conversion 1–2 weeks later.

Lead time: Earliest available signal — 6–8 weeks ahead of M1 retention.

How is the baseline calculated? ▼

Each market has its own rolling baseline computed as the 8-week trailing median of each signal, excluding the current week. The median is used instead of the mean to reduce sensitivity to outlier weeks (e.g., holiday weeks, launch campaigns).

Markets with fewer than 4 weeks of data (new markets) use a global peer baseline derived from similarly-sized markets at comparable stages of growth, until enough local history accumulates.

Baselines are recalculated weekly. A market whose baseline itself has been declining for 8 weeks will have a "compressed" baseline — this is intentional and visible in the trend sparkline, which shows the raw absolute values over time.

How are status thresholds (Critical / Watch / Stable) determined? ▼

Status is determined by a combination of composite score and signal concentration:

StatusComposite ScoreOR Signal Condition
CriticalBelow 50FTD conversion dropped >15% WoW, or 3+ signals simultaneously deteriorating
Watch50–691–2 signals below baseline by >1 std deviation, consistent for ≥2 weeks
Stable70–100All signals within normal variance (±1 std deviation of baseline)

The multi-signal condition matters: a single metric moving is noise; two or three moving together is signal. The composite score alone does not determine status — a market can be flagged Critical due to a single dramatic FTD drop even if other signals are healthy.

How are Region scores calculated? ▼

The region composite score is the size-weighted average of constituent market scores, where size weight is proportional to the number of active couriers in each market (approximated by cohort volume). In this prototype, it is a simple average.

Region status is determined by the worst constituent market: if any market in the region is Critical, the region is Critical. If any market is Watch, the region is Watch. All Stable → region is Stable.

Germany and Israel are shown as standalone regions (single-market) given their operational independence.

How should I act on this watchlist? ▼
What does "M1 Impact in X weeks" mean?

It estimates how many weeks until the current leading-indicator degradation will show up in your standard M1 retention metric, based on typical cohort flow. A market showing "1 week" is either already impacting M1 or will do so in the current reporting cycle.

What action should I take for a Critical market?

Each market's detail panel contains a Hypothesis (likely root cause derived from signal pattern) and a Recommended lever. These are starting points for investigation — verify the hypothesis with local data before deploying a lever. Escalate to the market operations owner within 48h of a Critical flag.

How do I know if an intervention worked?

The trend sparkline shows the 8-week rolling FTD conversion. If an intervention was deployed, you should see the trend stabilise or reverse within 2–3 weeks. Because the baseline is rolling, a sustained recovery will eventually raise the baseline — plan to revisit the threshold if the market consistently outperforms its old baseline.

The hypothesis seems wrong for my market — what should I do?

The hypotheses are auto-generated from signal patterns and are directional, not definitive. Use them as starting points. If your local knowledge contradicts the hypothesis, investigate both — you may be seeing a novel pattern. Feedback to the analytics team helps improve hypothesis quality over time.
